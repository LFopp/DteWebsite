Common reasons of Littering





Considering these factors and knowing that schools are relatively clean and well-maintained institutions, you wonder how littering is still an issue in schools. Here are some reasons why:

- Lack of trash bins

- Cleanliness of the site

- The behaviour of other people in the area

- If people can be seen littering

- Lack of information – Students litter because they don’t comprehend the gravity of the impact that litter causes.

- Presence of litter – Students are likelier to throw in unclean sites because there’s already litter in the area.

- Laziness – Students litter is because they’re too lazy to look for trash bins, and throwing trash anywhere is more convenient.

- Lax execution of rules on littering – people disregard rules when they are not imposed well, and when there is not enough signage to remind them.


Rate of waste thrown in schools


Food waste is the most commonly generated rubbish from schools. It accounted for approximately 24% of the total waste generated by schools. Food waste includes single-use plastic cups and utensils, paper and styrofoam takeout containers, and drink cans.

Recyclable paper products like cardboard and white and coloured paper comprised 23.5% of the total waste generated by schools. The remaining 50% of school rubbish was organic waste, such as non-recyclable paper items and leftover food.
